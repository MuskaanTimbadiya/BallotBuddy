About Ballot Buddy

Democracy thrives when its citizens are informed and engaged. Ballot Buddy is an initiative designed to simplify the complex election process in India. Whether you are a first-time voter learning how to register, or an experienced citizen verifying your polling booth, this guide provides clear, actionable, and official information.

Our objective is to ensure electoral participation is accessible to all eligible citizens, reinforcing the foundation of the world's largest democracy.

Why Your Vote Matters

Cases Where One Vote Changed History

Real stories. Real elections. Real consequences.

01 / 04
"My own wife couldn't vote."

Nathdwara, Rajasthan — 2008

62,216 Winner
VS
62,215 C.P. Joshi (INC)

C.P. Joshi, then-President of the Rajasthan Pradesh Congress Committee, lost by exactly 1 vote. His own wife and driver failed to cast their ballots that day.

02 / 04
"The future of a state, decided by a handful."

Arunachal Pradesh — 2009

44 Votes separated 2 candidates

In Daporijo constituency, a state assembly seat was decided by just 44 votes. In a constituency with thousands of eligible voters, over half did not show up — any one of them could have swung the result.

03 / 04
"One seat changed who runs the country."

Bihar — 1999 General Elections

7 Seats majority margin

The Vajpayee government fell by a single-seat margin in 1999 — triggering snap elections. Multiple constituencies in Bihar were won or lost by fewer than 500 votes, which collectively tipped the national balance of power.

04 / 04
"A tie broken by a coin toss."

Himachal Pradesh — 2017

0 Votes difference

In Lahaul & Spiti, the election resulted in a perfect tie. The winner was decided by a draw of lots. An entire constituency's fate was left to chance — because enough voters stayed home.
